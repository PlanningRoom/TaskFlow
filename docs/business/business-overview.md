# TaskFlow — Business Overview

## The Elevator Pitch

Every team has work to manage, but most task management tools either do too little or overwhelm with complexity. TaskFlow sits in the sweet spot — it gives teams a clear, structured way to organize their work without burying them in features they don't need. It's simple enough to pick up in minutes, but powerful enough to keep a team of fifty people on the same page.

---

## What Is TaskFlow?

TaskFlow is a collaborative task management application built for teams that want to stay organized without adopting heavyweight project management software. It provides a shared workspace where team members can create projects, break work down into tasks, and track progress together in real time.

At its core, TaskFlow answers three questions every team faces daily: *What needs to be done? Who's doing it? Where does it stand?*

## Who Is TaskFlow For?

TaskFlow is designed for teams of 10 to 50 people. These are teams large enough to need structure — where sticky notes and group chats no longer cut it — but not so large that they need enterprise-grade project management with dozens of configuration options.

TaskFlow doesn't target any specific industry. Whether the team is building software, planning events, running a marketing campaign, or coordinating research, the fundamentals of task management are the same: create tasks, assign them, move them through a process, and communicate along the way.

## How Does It Work?

### Workspaces and Projects

Everything in TaskFlow lives inside a workspace. A workspace is a team's shared home — the place where all their projects, tasks, and conversations come together. Within a workspace, teams organize their efforts into projects. A project might represent a product launch, a client engagement, a sprint, or any body of work that a group of people needs to coordinate around.

### Tasks and Workflow

Inside each project, work is captured as tasks. A task has a title, a description, labels for categorization, and an assignee — the person responsible for getting it done. Every task moves through a clear, consistent workflow that reflects how work actually progresses:

- **Backlog** — the idea has been captured but hasn't been planned yet
- **To Do** — the work is planned and ready for someone to pick up
- **In Progress** — someone is actively working on it
- **In Review** — the work is done but needs a second set of eyes before it can be called complete
- **Done** — finished

Sometimes work gets abandoned. Rather than deleting those tasks and losing the history, they can be marked as **Cancelled** — acknowledged as something the team chose not to pursue.

This workflow is intentionally the same across every project. Consistency means that anyone moving between projects immediately understands where things stand, and the team always has a shared language for describing the state of their work.

### Collaboration

Task management is a team activity, and TaskFlow is built around that reality. Team members can comment directly on tasks to discuss details, ask questions, and share updates. When someone needs a specific person's attention, they can @mention them, which triggers a notification so nothing falls through the cracks.

Updates happen in real time. When a teammate moves a task, adds a comment, or changes an assignment, everyone sees it immediately — no refreshing, no wondering whether they're looking at stale information. An activity feed provides a running timeline of what's been happening across the project, so anyone can catch up quickly after time away.

### Roles and Access

Not everyone on a team needs the same level of access, and TaskFlow reflects that with four simple roles:

- **Owner** — the person who created the workspace. They have full control and the final say on who's in and who's out.
- **Admin** — a trusted manager who handles day-to-day operations like inviting new members, adjusting roles, and overseeing projects.
- **Member** — the working contributor. Members create tasks, update their status, comment, and collaborate.
- **Viewer** — a read-only participant. Viewers can see everything they've been given access to, but they observe rather than contribute. This is ideal for stakeholders, clients, or executives who want visibility without the risk of accidental changes.

Access goes a step further at the project level. Not every person in a workspace needs to see every project. Admins can control which users have access to which projects, keeping things focused and relevant.

### Joining a Workspace

TaskFlow uses an invite-only model. There's no public signup page — people join when an existing member invites them. This keeps the workspace intentional. Every person in the system is someone the team chose to include, whether that's a full-time contributor or an external stakeholder who needs visibility into a specific project.

## What Makes TaskFlow Different?

TaskFlow's strength is in what it chooses *not* to do. It doesn't try to be a project planning tool, a time tracker, a reporting suite, or an integration hub. It focuses entirely on the daily mechanics of getting work done as a team — capturing tasks, moving them forward, and keeping everyone informed.

This focus makes it fast to learn, easy to use, and hard to outgrow within its intended scope. There are no configuration mazes, no workflow builders, and no dashboards that require a training session to understand. A new team member can be productive within their first session.

## Accessibility and Inclusivity

TaskFlow is designed to be usable by everyone. It meets established accessibility standards, ensuring that people who navigate with keyboards, use screen readers, or have visual impairments can use the application fully. Complex interface elements like task boards include alternative views so that no one is excluded from participating.

The application launches in English but is built from the ground up to support additional languages in the future. Dates, times, and numbers automatically display in the format that's familiar to each user based on their location and preferences.

## How It's Delivered

TaskFlow is a web application. There's nothing to install — users access it through their browser on any device, whether that's a desktop computer, a laptop, a tablet, or a phone. The interface adapts to the screen it's being viewed on, so the experience is comfortable regardless of device size.

The application is hosted in the cloud and managed centrally, so teams never have to worry about updates, backups, or infrastructure. Everyone is always on the latest version.

## Privacy

TaskFlow collects only the information it needs to function. Users can delete their accounts and personal data at any time. A clear privacy policy explains what data is stored and where, giving teams confidence that their information is handled responsibly.

---

TaskFlow is task management stripped down to what matters — a clear, shared view of the work, the people doing it, and the progress being made. Nothing more, nothing less.
