# Decision 026: Design Example Themes

**Status:** Decided

**Category:** Mockup Scope

**Question:** The mockup website will present 3 or more design examples. What visual directions should be explored? Candidates include: minimal/dark (Linear-inspired), friendly/colorful (Asana-inspired), enterprise/dense (Jira-inspired), soft/modern (Notion-inspired), or other directions.

**Decision:** Three design examples were explored, all within the minimal & soft modern personality:

1. **Light & Airy** — White/near-white backgrounds, very subtle shadows, maximum whitespace, cool gray tones. Closest to Notion/Linear.
2. **Warm Neutral** — Off-white and warm gray backgrounds, slightly more prominent card shadows, warmer tones. Feels grounded and approachable.
3. **Soft Contrast** — Light gray background with white cards for clear layering, bolder typography weights, stronger visual hierarchy through contrast.

**Selected: Warm Neutral** paired with the **Ocean Teal** color palette. This combination provides the warmth and approachability that best reflects TaskFlow's brand personality while the teal accent brings a professional, calming energy distinct from the semantic color system.

**Rationale:** The three variations were built as an interactive mockup (mockup.html) with switchable design options and color palettes. Warm Neutral was selected because it strikes the best balance — warmer than Light & Airy (which felt too sparse) and less heavy than Soft Contrast (which felt too structured for a tool that serves both daily users and occasional viewers). Ocean Teal was selected over Indigo Dusk (too common) and Dusk Violet (too playful) for its professionalism and clear separation from the status/priority color system.
